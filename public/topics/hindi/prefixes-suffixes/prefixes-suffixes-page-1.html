Common Hindi Prefixes (उपसर्ग)

Prefixes in Hindi are called "उपसर्ग" (upsarg). They are added before a word to modify its meaning. Hindi has prefixes from both Sanskrit (तत्सम) and native Hindi (तद्भव) origins.

Prefix Meaning Example
अ / अन् Negation, without, not अशुद्ध (impure), अनंत (endless)
सु Good, well, nice सुगंध (fragrance), सुविधा (convenience)
दुर् / दु Bad, difficult दुर्गंध (bad smell), दुर्घटना (accident)
प्र Forward, forth, very प्रगति (progress), प्रसिद्ध (famous)
अधि Over, above, superior अधिकार (authority), अधिपति (lord)
More Examples:
निर्दोष (faultless) परिवार (family) विदेश (foreign country) उपवन (garden) संयोग (coincidence)

Common Hindi Suffixes (प्रत्यय)

Suffixes in Hindi are called "प्रत्यय" (pratyay). They are added after a word to modify its meaning or grammatical function. Suffixes can change nouns to adjectives, create abstract nouns, or modify verbs.

Suffix Function Example
-ता / -त्व Forms abstract nouns सुंदरता (beauty), मानवत्व (humanity)
-वाला Indicates possession or profession दूधवाला (milkman), दिल्लीवाला (person from Delhi)
-कर Forms adverbs धीरेकर (slowly), जोरकर (forcefully)
-इक Forms adjectives ऐतिहासिक (historical), वैज्ञानिक (scientific)
-ई Forms feminine nouns or adjectives लड़की (girl), भारतीय (Indian)
More Examples:
मिठाई (sweet) बचपन (childhood) लिखावट (handwriting) चालाक (clever) गरीबी (poverty)

Word Formation Rules

When adding prefixes and suffixes to Hindi words, certain phonological changes may occur:

  1. When a prefix ending in a consonant meets a root beginning with a vowel, a sandhi (sound change) may occur.
  2. Some prefixes change form slightly depending on the first letter of the root word.
  3. Adding certain suffixes may cause the final vowel of the root word to change.
Sandhi Examples:
उत् + आहार = उदाहरण (example) सम् + आचार = सदाचार (good conduct) निर् + अर्थ = निरर्थक (meaningless)
