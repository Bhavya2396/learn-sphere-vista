उपसर्ग और प्रत्यय (Prefixes and Suffixes)

A comprehensive guide to Hindi word formation through prefixes and suffixes

Introduction

Hindi, like many other languages, uses prefixes and suffixes to modify words and create new meanings. Understanding these building blocks is essential for expanding vocabulary and mastering the language.

Key Concept

In Hindi grammar, prefixes are called उपसर्ग (upsarg) and suffixes are called प्रत्यय (pratyay).

Hindi Prefixes (उपसर्ग)

Prefixes are added before the root word to modify its meaning. Hindi has both native (तत्सम) and borrowed (तद्भव) prefixes.

Common Hindi Prefixes

Prefix Meaning Example
अ Negation, absence अशुद्ध (impure) - शुद्ध (pure)
अन Negation, absence अनपढ़ (illiterate) - पढ़ (read)
सु Good, well सुगंध (fragrance) - गंध (smell)
दुर् Bad, difficult दुर्गंध (bad smell) - गंध (smell)
प्र Forward, forth प्रगति (progress) - गति (movement)
अधि Over, above अधिकार (authority) - कार (action)
अनु After, following अनुवाद (translation) - वाद (speech)
अप Away, bad अपमान (insult) - मान (honor)
Example Usage

अज्ञान (ajñān) - Ignorance

Root: ज्ञान (jñān) - Knowledge

Prefix: अ (a) - Negation

Meaning: Lack of knowledge or ignorance

Hindi Suffixes (प्रत्यय)

Suffixes are added after the root word to modify its meaning or change its part of speech. Hindi has a rich system of suffixes.

Common Hindi Suffixes

Suffix Usage Example
वाला Denotes occupation or possession दूधवाला (milkman) - दूध (milk)
ई Forms abstract nouns मिठाई (sweetness) - मीठा (sweet)
ता Forms abstract nouns सुंदरता (beauty) - सुंदर (beautiful)
आई Forms abstract nouns बड़ाई (greatness) - बड़ा (big)
आना Forms verbs सुखाना (to dry) - सूखा (dry)
दार Possessor or holder ज़मींदार (landlord) - ज़मीन (land)
पन Forms abstract nouns बचपन (childhood) - बच्चा (child)
Example Usage

सच्चाई (saccāī) - Truth

Root: सच (sac) - True

Suffix: आई (āī) - Forms abstract noun

Meaning: The quality of being true or truthfulness

Word Formation Rules

When combining prefixes and suffixes with root words, certain phonological and morphological rules apply:

  1. Some prefixes may change form when attached to certain roots (e.g., दुर् + गंध = दुर्गंध)
  2. Some root words may undergo slight changes when suffixes are added (e.g., मीठा + ई = मिठाई)
  3. Sometimes both prefixes and suffixes can be added to the same root (e.g., अ + विद्या + आर्थी = अविद्यार्थी)
Practice Tip

A good way to practice is to take a root word and try to add different prefixes and suffixes to create new words. Then check their meanings in a dictionary.

Summary

Understanding prefixes and suffixes in Hindi helps you:

  • Expand your vocabulary efficiently
  • Recognize word relationships
  • Understand the nuances of word meanings
  • Improve your reading comprehension
  • Enhance your ability to express yourself in Hindi

Continue practicing with different combinations to master this important aspect of Hindi word formation.
